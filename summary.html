The Problem in 30 Seconds

◆ Where Victoria is today

Victoria's operating budget is up 29% in three years ($297.9M → $384.1M). Property tax: 6.15% → 7.93% → 6.99%, with 10.44% proposed for 2026. VicPD now costs $750 per resident — the highest in BC. Missing Middle delivered 61 permits in 2024 against a target of 150. 48% of downtown businesses would leave if their lease expired. $25M spent on homelessness since 2023 — with no published outcome metrics.

No competitive benchmarking. No AI. No digital twin. No smart infrastructure. No zero-based reviews.

Victoria does not need to spend more. It needs to spend smarter.

The Flagship Measures

🏠 Liveable

  • 12,000+ new homes by 2030 with composition targets: 25% missing middle, 20% 3BR+, 15% non-market, 30% rental
  • City land bank — strategic acquisition and competitive disposition of City-owned land (v1.2, continuing Alto 2022)
  • Published permit times by category: simple <6 weeks, complex <6 months, rezoning <9 months
  • Adaptive traffic signals on major corridors (Pittsburgh evidence: −25% travel time, −21% emissions)
  • Champion Westshore rapid transit and the E&N corridor — endorse LRT/BRT, protect right-of-way
  • First-hour free parking in commercial areas + real-time parking availability
  • STEP-style supportive-housing throughput with BC Housing + Island Health + Pacifica — free up the supportive units, not just add to them (v1.1)
  • Short-term rental licensing tightening to protect the long-term rental pool (v1.1)
  • Reach Victoria's young adults (18–25) — youth-priority space, late-evening hours, post-secondary partnerships (v1.2)
  • Universal accessibility audit and remediation — every sidewalk, public building, washroom, transit-adjacent site (v1.2)

🛡️ Safe

  • Downtown Public Order Team (DPOT) — unified VicPD + Bylaw + Sanitation + Outreach, single daily briefing
  • Bylaw enforcement 6 AM – 10 PM, 7 days a week (vs. current 7 AM – 4 PM)
  • Smart LED streetlights — Kelowna/Halifax model. No audio surveillance. No behavioural AI. Self-financing in 3–4 years
  • Resolve the VicPD funding dispute with Esquimalt — transparent service-consumption formula, binding mediation if needed
  • Greater Victoria Regional Policing Services Review — 18-month co-sponsored review with binding mediation outcome (v1.1)
  • Phased capacity-first enforcement: enforce when there is somewhere to send people, not when there isn't
  • Drug-use buffer zones around schools, playgrounds, libraries, and community centres (v1.1)

🌳 Beautiful

  • Modernize underground utility GIS (M53) — coordinate excavation, reduce re-trenched streets, federal modernization-grant eligible (v1.3 reframe of the Digital Twin)
  • One-time LIDAR scan of downtown core (M53c) — heritage protection, emergency-management baseline, planning-support data layer (v1.3)
  • AI-generated public consultation imagery (M53b) — continuing Coun. Dell's Beacon Hill precedent, on every meaningful public project
  • Heritage protection + restored street furniture — Victoria's character is its competitive advantage
  • Cultural-venue preservation tool — right-of-first-refusal + lease-bridge fund for designated live-music and arts spaces (v1.1)
  • Pedestrianization pilots — Bastion Square full, Government Street seasonal (v1.2)
  • Royal Athletic Park restoration — reverse the 2025 reserve raid and complete deferred upgrades (v1.2, continuing Alto 2022)
  • 5,000 new trees by 2030 — climate-resilient species, free seedlings for homeowners
  • −50% community GHG by 2030 (19 points still needed from current −31%)
  • Maximum-temperature rental bylaw (26°C) in at least one room during declared heat-warning periods (v1.1)

💰 Well-Managed

  • Managed competition for waste and street cleaning — ~$2.4M/year savings, workers protected by mandatory re-employment clauses (Phoenix precedent: $25M+ saved 1978–1988)
  • Zero-based budget review of all 200+ programs on a rotating cycle (Calgary SAVE: $74M targeted by 2022; $60M actually found per March 2026)
  • Tax glide path: Year 1 ≤6.5%, Year 2 ≤5%, Year 3+ ≈CPI + population growth (3.5%) — versus the council's 10.44% draft
  • Vacant storefront strategy: advocate to Province for enabling legislation + use existing levers (licensing, façade conditions, pop-up facilitation) + quarterly transparency report
  • Confront provincial downloading — quarterly public ledger of unfunded mandates + formal refusal threshold (v1.1)
  • Rationalize City real estate — sell or lease underused properties; proceeds to debt reduction and capital

🗳️ Democratic

  • Referendum on every new (non-lifecycle) capital project over $25M — within BC LGA/Community Charter framework (Assent Voting or AAP)
  • Settle Saanich amalgamation through a binding Citizens' Assembly (60 randomly selected residents, 12–18 months, both councils pre-commit to honour the result)
  • Integrity Commissioner office + MNP governance report implementation tracker (v1.1)
  • Quarterly performance dashboard — permits, potholes, crime, housing, budget variance — published, no spin
  • Government-to-government working tables with the Songhees Nation and the Esquimalt Nation, on standing quarterly cadence

How Every Major New Project Gets Decided

  1. Consult locally — what residents say needs to improve
  2. Design with experts — engineers, planners, affected residents
  3. Visualize and analyze impact — AI imagery (M53b) and GIS analysis (M53) show the future before construction
  4. Referendum if >$25M (new, non-lifecycle capital), under BC statutory process
  5. Report quarterly during construction — on time, on budget, or explain why not
