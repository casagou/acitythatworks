◆ What this is
A side-by-side comparison of the Victoria 2026 mayoral and council field, scored against the ~100 commitments of the framework. Each row is a policy area; the scoring tells you at a glance who supports what — and where the framework is alone in proposing a specific mechanism. Not a slate. Not an attack piece. Single-line ideas inevitably flatten nuance; use this as a starting point, not the final word.

How to Read This

✅ Aligned — has publicly supported a position substantively similar to the framework's, in voting record or stated platform
🟡 Partial — some alignment but with reservations, a different mechanism, or weaker formulation
❌ Opposed — has publicly opposed or voted against a similar position
⚪ Silent — no public position found; treat as unknown rather than opposed

Every score is backed by a public source: voting record (council minutes, major-outlet reporting), declared platform, or direct public statement. Where no public position exists, the cell is marked silent rather than guessed. Silence is not opposition.

Candidate Profiles

Full alignment detail per candidate. Sources cited inline in the master record.

Marianne Alto — Mayor, declared May 20, 2026

Tier: Incumbent, 55.5% of vote in 2022. Tagline: "Experience that delivers."

Agrees with the framework on: regional policing consolidation (M29b absorbed her plank); permissive tax exemptions for not-for-profit clinics (2022, still undelivered, absorbed into M13b); city land bank (2022); rent banks; façade improvement grants; provincial downloading frame (M70b); lək̓ʷəŋən place names + cultural protocols.

Diverges on: No ZBB in three years (budget grew 29%); no published permit benchmarks; no managed-competition consideration; no quarterly dashboard; no Integrity Commissioner / MNP implementation; Dowler Place process ("we knew what the comments would be"); CSWB Phase 1 funded by raiding reserves.

Distinctive Alto ideas worth considering: permissive tax exemption for purpose-built rentals; family-doctor incentives at scale; tax relief for community-serving groups; co-located firehall + housing + health-services civic infrastructure.

Jeremy Caradonna — Councillor, declared Dec 21, 2025

Tier: First to declare. Topped polls 2022 with 14,238 votes. CRD vice-chair.

Agrees on: Missing Middle overhaul mechanics (his Sept 2023 drafting); sponsored the VicPD 2026 patrol-officer cut (4→2) as progressive fiscal restraint; co-author cooling bylaw (M64); Caledonia Place regional fair-share motion with Hammond (M70b).

Diverges on: managed competition; no published permit benchmarks; no quarterly dashboard; YES on overall 2026 budget.

Notable quote: "Victoria will likely look more like a miniature London or Paris than a miniature Vancouver or Manhattan."

Matt Dell — Councillor, declared Dec 31, 2025

Tier: 13,899 votes in 2022. 15 years drafting policy for BC government.

Agrees on: cooling bylaw co-author (M64); AI public consultation imagery pioneer (Beacon Hill All-Weather Field — directly absorbed as M53b); Hermann's Jazz Club preservation (absorbed as M46b); strongly pro-housing volume; annual line-by-line budget review.

Diverges on: managed competition; pro-budget-growth framing differs from the glide path; no permit benchmarks; no quarterly dashboard.

Notable quote: "The epitome of bad governance is the failure to make a decision."

Strategic note: Most operationally-curious progressive on council. v1.1 absorbed three of his specific ideas. Natural ally on process measures even if divergent on fiscal posture.

Dave Thompson — Councillor, declared

Agrees on: Hoboken Vision Zero benchmark cited publicly — same peer-city evidence the framework uses; co-author cooling bylaw; co-author Coordinated Access & Assessment downtown prioritization (June 2025); eliminate parking minimums; e-bike rebates; YES on Caradonna's VicPD ask cut.

Diverges on: managed competition; YES on 2026 budget (different framing than the glide path); no permit benchmarks.

Distinctive idea now absorbed: federal SUV/truck hood-height advocacy — now M42b.

Susan Kim — Councillor, undecided

Status: No 2026 declaration as of May 26. Director of Operations, Fernwood NRG.

Diverges on: managed competition (committed against P3s); lone NO on July 2024 daytime sheltering ban (vs. M33).

Strategic note: Three of her 2022 platform ideas are now in v1.1. The framework absorbs her system critique while diverging on enforcement.

Krista Loughton — Councillor, undecided

Agrees on: STEP (Supporting Tenants, Enabling Pathways) motion (Jan 2026) — absorbed directly into M11. Single best individual idea on the council table; co-author Coordinated Access & Assessment; Renters' Hub; flagged "the staggering cost of our current direction" honestly.

Diverges on: managed competition; YES on 2026 budget; no permit benchmark mechanism.

Strategic note: Measure 11 now explicitly absorbs her STEP work.

Chris Coleman — Councillor, undecided (June/July decision)

Methodology & Caveats

Scope and time-stamp. Current as of May 26, 2026 — 144 days before the October 17, 2026 election. Nomination doesn't open until September 1. Several candidates are undecided. Positions will shift.

Scoring approach. Each cell reflects the most recent public position found in voting record, declared platform, or direct public statement. Where reporting is contradictory, the most recent on-the-record source wins.

Why silent is not opposed. A candidate not having taken a public position is not the same as opposing it. Treating silence as opposition would be a false negative.

Why this matrix is framework-anchored, not neutral. Rows are framework measures; scoring is against framework positions. This framework is the only platform in the field with ~100 specific, costed commitments — there is no other framework with that density to anchor against. The explicit purpose is to show where the framework stands alone on a specific mechanism. Readers seeking a different anchor are invited to draft one; this framework is open.

This document is open to correction. Spot an error in scoring, a misread of a public position, or a position that has evolved? Write to info@acitythatworks.ca. Revisions are dated and published.
