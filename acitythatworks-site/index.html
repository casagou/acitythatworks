A Citizens' Framework◆Victoria, BC · 2026◆v1.3 · May 27, 2026

A city that works.

Security. Cleanliness. Results.

What should we hope for our city? What do we have the right to demand — for ourselves and for our children?

The time for complaining is over. The moment has come to imagine a city that works — right now: to want it with all our strength, and to build it together, with realism and with method.

A City That Works is an independent, non-partisan policy framework for the City of Victoria, drafted by residents who want practical results from their local government. It is 0% ideological, 100% pragmatic — its only loyalty is to what works elsewhere and can work here.

This is not a party. There are no whips, no candidate slate, no exclusivity — just a public menu of evidence-based municipal policies, costed and sourced, open for adoption by any candidate.

◆ How this framework was built

0% ideology. 100% pragmatic.

We started where the truth is most visible: the budget. Politicians make promises; numbers don't lie. Victoria's operating budget grew 29% in three years, from $297.9M to $384.1M proposed — with over 53% going to salaries and benefits, VicPD's budget nearly doubling in a decade, and no competitive benchmarking, AI, or smart infrastructure deployed. For every problem a Victorian faces daily, we applied the same six-step method.

01
Identify the root cause
Not the symptom — the structural reason the problem exists.
02
Assess what the City can actually do
Municipalities have specific powers. Don't promise what requires provincial or federal action.
03
Study what works elsewhere
Pittsburgh's Surtrac: −25% travel time, −21% emissions. Phoenix managed competition: $25M+ saved 1978–88. Calgary SAVE: $74M targeted, $60M found.
04
Measure with technology
Use data, sensors, and digital tools to understand before deciding.
05
Build solutions with practitioners
Engineers, police, builders, social workers, business owners, neighbourhood associations, residents.
06
Cost every measure
Direct and indirect impacts, costs and revenues. Every single proposal has a budget impact estimate.
◆ The 5-Step Process

No major new public project without these five steps.

The Dowler Place file proved why this matters. Under this framework, no project skips any step.

STEP 01
Local consultation
Problems, pain points, what needs to improve.
STEP 02
Expert design
Engineers, planners, affected residents.
STEP 03
Visualize & analyze
AI imagery (M53b) + GIS analysis (M53). See the effects before spending.
STEP 04
Referendum if > $25M
Binding public vote on new (non-lifecycle) capital.
STEP 05
Quarterly reports
On time, on budget, or explain why not.
◆ Separation of Powers

What the City actually controls.

Before making promises, we must be honest about what the City of Victoria actually controls. Many of the problems residents face fall under provincial or federal jurisdiction. We advocate aggressively to senior governments for action in their domains — but we never promise what isn't ours to deliver. See the full plain-language guide →

City of Victoria controls
Land use & zoning · building permits · property taxes · bylaws & enforcement · municipal policing (via VicPD Board) · parks & recreation · local roads & sidewalks · water & sewer · garbage collection · local planning · business licences · public art & heritage designation
Province of BC controls
Healthcare · K-12 education · social housing funding (BC Housing) · welfare & income assistance · courts & sentencing · major transit (BC Transit) · highways · building code · vacancy & speculation taxes
Federal government controls
Immigration · criminal law · employment insurance · Old Age Security · major infrastructure grants · Indigenous affairs (Crown-Nation relationship) · ports & harbours

◆ Principles

What we are — and what we're not.

We are
  • →Govern like managers — measure, adjust, deliver
  • →Consult before building — and listen to the answer
  • →Visualize before constructing — AI imagery and GIS analysis, not demolition
  • →Protect the environment with results, not slogans
  • →Enforce the rules that exist — before inventing new ones
  • →Respect taxpayers — every dollar audited, every quarter reported
  • →Cost every promise — no measure without a budget line
We aren't
  • →We don't do ideology — we do results
  • →We don't make promises we can't measure
  • →We don't pick fights — we fix problems
  • →We don't spend first and justify later — taxpayers decide on big new projects
  • →We don't try things in real life when we can simulate them first
  • →We don't deploy public surveillance — better lighting, not behavioural AI
